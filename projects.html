Sanction & Embargo Monitoring Pipeline

An end-to-end monitoring pipeline designed to detect data drift, reduce false positives, and improve sanctions alert reliability.

Organizer: Société Générale — Global Solution Center, India

Problem

Financial institutions process large volumes of transactions that must be screened against global sanctions and embargo lists. Traditional screening systems generate a high number of false positives, forcing analysts to manually review alerts and increasing operational overhead while still posing compliance risk. SG wanted to develop a model to screen transaction alerts after analysts' reviews to guardrail any human error that causes hefty fines. SG also wanted to ensure the ongoing reliability, accuracy of the system by continously tracking data quality, operational performance

Approach

1. Adapted and retrained a ML model for two client types: Individuals and Corporate.
- Individuals: Employed a rule-based approach combined with text similarity metrics (specifically Jaro distance) to assess the resemblance between client names and names on sanction lists. The model calculates edit distances and applies thresholds to determine if an alert is a potential match.
- Corporates: Developed a machine learning model using the Extra Trees classifier. Employed oversampling for "True" labeled alerts to address class imbalance. Conducted hyperparameter tuning(grid search over number of estimators, max depth etc) to optimize model precision and finalized the best performing model. 2. Model Monitoring: Implemented a model monitoring framework tracking 6+ KPIs as drift indicators. The monitoring framework provided robust, automated oversight of the QATP alert screening system. By proactively identifying data quality issues, operational anomalies, and inconsistencies, this framework ensured the model's outputs remained reliable and compliant with regulatory standards without regular manual review.

Impact

↓ 25% false positives • 6+ KPIs continuously monitored

Automated Anomaly Classification using GEN AI

AI-driven system for automated anomaly categorization and domain level risk summarization.

Organizer: Société Générale — Global Solution Center, India

Problem

Risk teams manually categorized 8k+ multilingual anomalies annually, consuming weeks of repetitive effort.

Solution

- Designed a rule based classification pipeline augmented with semantic similarity and clustering techniques.
- Used fin-mpnet-base embeddings to convert anomaly descriptions into dense vectors for semantic comparison.
- Applied clustering to identify dominant anomaly patterns within each risk domain.
- Leveraged GPT-4 to generate structured, domain-level synthesis documents summarizing key risks and trends

Impact

- 1.45 FTE reduction in manual effort
- ~2,900 analyst hours saved annually

AML Threshold Optimization & Calibration

Transaction monitoring systems rely on thresholds to balance risk coverage and alert volume. Poorly calibrated thresholds lead to excessive false positives, increasing analyst workload without improving risk detection.

Organizer: Société Générale — Global Solution Center, India

Approach

I led a statistical threshold calibration initiative across multiple AML scenarios and legal entities to reduce false positives while maintaining regulatory risk coverage. Performed distribution analysis and quantile-based calibration across transaction scenarios. Conducted sensitivity trade-off evaluations to understand the impact of threshold changes on alert volume and coverage.

Impact

↓ 25% alert volume across 16 entities • 30+ scenarios calibrated • 1.5 FTE efficiency gain
